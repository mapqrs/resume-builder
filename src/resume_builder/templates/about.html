What this tool is

It turns a single source-of-truth master resume into three polished outputs — a job-specific tailored .docx (or PDF), a matching cover letter, and a LinkedIn profile — in under five minutes per application.

And it never invents experience, projects, or metrics. A fabrication guard runs on every output and drops anything the source doesn't support.

Why a resume still matters

Recruiters spend ~6 seconds on a first-pass review (Bock, Work Rules). Whatever they pull out of those six seconds — your last role, a number, a brand name — is the entire shortlist signal. Everything else gets read only if those six seconds land.

ATS systems (Workday / Greenhouse / Lever / India's TalentRecruit and Naukri RMS) score your resume against the JD before any human sees it. Keyword overlap and clean structure are mandatory. Visual flair is irrelevant — the ATS strips it.

What to check before you submit

  • Every claim is true. If you can't defend a metric in an interview, drop it. This tool's anti-fabrication guard catches invented numbers/tools — but you should re-read for nuance the guard can't see (e.g., "led" vs "contributed to").
  • ATS keyword coverage. The web UI shows a coverage percentage (green ≥80%, yellow 60-79%, red <60%). Below 60% usually means you're filtered out before a human reads it.
  • Strong verbs, no clichés. "Led", "shipped", "built", "scaled" beat "responsible for", "worked on", "passionate about". The style-lints panel flags these.
  • Length matches the role. One page for 0-10 years; two pages for 8+ with genuine breadth. Don't pad to fill space.
  • Numbers wherever possible. Bock's "XYZ" rule: accomplished X as measured by Y by doing Z. Resumes with numbers clear ATS + recruiter filters at 2-3× the rate.
  • Tailored, not generic. The same master → 3 roles = 3 different tailored resumes. Resist the urge to send one generic version everywhere.

What not to obsess over

  • Visual design. Photo, color scheme, custom fonts, sidebars, two-column layouts — ATS systems flatten or misparse all of these. Stick to a clean linear layout.
  • Address (street). City + country is enough. Recruiters do not need your full address; some ATS systems flag it as a privacy concern.
  • Hobbies / interests section. Bock: "a trophy without context is noise" applies here too. Skip unless it signals something concrete (Open Source maintainer, athletics at national level, etc.).
  • Photo. India and US conventions diverge: in India, photos are sometimes expected (especially in legacy sectors). For tech / global roles, leave it off — many ATSes filter resumes with embedded images.
  • References available on request. Implicit. Don't take up a line.
  • Objective statement. Replaced by the summary (one line, role-relevant) decades ago.

Why this tool specifically

  • Anti-fabrication moat. Most AI resume tools cheerfully invent numbers to "make you sound stronger." Recruiters smell it; if they probe and you can't defend the metric, you're out. Our guard rejects any number or proper noun not in your master's vocabulary.
  • Bock alignment. Laszlo Bock ran hiring at Google for a decade and wrote the book (literally) on how recruiters read resumes. Our defaults — XYZ bullets, page-count caps, "trophy + criteria" awards, salutation lints — are pulled directly from Work Rules.
  • Local-only, by default. Your data lives on your machine. The only network call is to Anthropic when you click Generate, with your own credentials. Privacy footer on every page reminds you of this.
  • India-first defaults. CGPA out of 10, percentage marks, "first class with distinction" all work in the GPA field. Indian role conventions baked into the JD-signal extractor and sample fixtures.
  • Three outputs from one source. Master → resume + cover letter + LinkedIn. You maintain one structured document; the tool keeps the other two in sync.

Where this helps

  • Job applications. Pick a JD, click Generate. Done in under a minute when claude CLI is logged in.
  • LinkedIn profile updates. Step 10 in the wizard generates Headline / About / Experience / Featured / Skills / Education as copy-paste blocks.
  • Cover letters. Bock-aligned 4-paragraph structure with salutation lint + company-specificity lint.
  • Career retrospection. The wizard's chunk-by-chunk brain-dump is itself useful, independent of tailoring — you walk away with a structured memory of every accomplishment you can defend.
  • Cross-checking your existing resume. Import an old PDF/DOCX into the wizard; the tool extracts the text and you re-organize from there.

How to start

  1. Run the wizard at /wizard and follow the 10 steps top-to-bottom. About 30 minutes the first time.
  2. Or copy samples/master.example.yaml to master.yaml and edit by hand if you'd rather skip the wizard. The sample is India-first with CGPA + INR examples.
  3. Open the tailor, paste your JD (or pick "No JD — describe target role"), choose a format preset, click Generate.
  4. Re-read. The ATS coverage / guard warnings / style lints panels surface the things worth fixing.

Privacy in one line

Your data stays on this machine. Prompts go to Anthropic only when you click Generate — with your own claude CLI login or your own ANTHROPIC_API_KEY. Nothing else leaves your laptop. No telemetry, no account system, no "anonymous usage stats."

The Delete my data link in the footer wipes sessions/, every master.yaml.bak.*, and legacy drafts/. Your current master.yaml is left alone.
