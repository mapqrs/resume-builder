      6. Education
      
    
        Add each degree, certification, or programme separately. Pick the
        status that matches reality — dropout, on-leave, deferred
        admit, and certifications-only are first-class options. India-aware:
        CGPA out of 10, percentage, “first class”, and US-style
        4-point GPA all work in the GPA field.
      
    

      
    
        Bock's GPA rule: include only if you're in the top
        quartile of your cohort — roughly ≥ 8.5/10 CGPA, ≥ 80%,
        first class with distinction, or ≥ 3.5/4 GPA. It is fine to
        slice (“3.7 in major”) but disclose it. Below the bar?
        Leave the GPA blank.
      
    

      
    
        Awards need criteria. “Smith Prize”
        alone is noise; “Smith Prize, top of cohort of 240”
        signals. Add the criteria for every award you include.
      
    

      
    
        Position-of-strength framing. Non-standard
        statuses (dropout, declined admit, on leave, deferred) get an extra
        textarea to capture why in your own voice. Say no to an
        elite MBA to scale a startup? Left undergrad early to ship
        something? That story belongs here — recruiters read it as
        decisiveness, not as a gap.
      
    

      
    

      
    
    

    
    
    
      7. Your basics
      
    
        The header of every resume: your name, contact info, and a few
        links. Only the name is required — everything else is optional
        but recruiters expect at least an email + phone.

      10. LinkedIn profile
      
    
        Generate a complete LinkedIn profile from everything you've captured.
        Each section is crafted for LinkedIn's medium (longer-form, first-person
        in About, keyword-rich) rather than reusing resume bullets verbatim.
        Same anti-fabrication guard: every claim traces back to a master bullet.
      
    

      
    
        Output is copy-paste blocks — no LinkedIn API integration.
        Paste each block into LinkedIn's edit screen for that section.
